Chiedere è un' abilità

Riflessioni su un gesto che sembra semplice ma non lo è

8 May 2026 3 min read Psicologia Crescita personale Relazioni familiari
✦

Penso che chiedere sia un atto importante. Fin da piccolo facevo molte richieste, e spesso ricevevo risposte brusche dai miei genitori. Non erano cattivi — probabilmente erano stressati ed esausti e facevano del loro meglio. Ma quello stress costante mi ha fatto crescere con la convinzione che non si possano chiedere favori agli altri.

Quando chiedevo qualcosa, venivo respinto. Eppure, guardandoli oggi, mio padre è sempre stato disponibile e non mi ha mai fatto mancare nulla. Questo mi fa pensare che il blocco non riguardasse la disponibilità concreta dei miei genitori, ma qualcosa di più sottile: forse il modo in cui certe richieste, in certi momenti, venivano vissute come un peso.

Ricordo una volta in macchina con mio padre: non riuscivo a chiedergli dieci euro per uscire. Ero completamente congelato. Eppure lui non avrebbe detto di no — lo so. Quel freeze non era razionale: era un riflesso condizionato, costruito nel tempo.

The Problem With Self-Typing

When I was young, I was really shy but loved computers. When I took the test, I was going through a period when I didn't have many friends, though I wasn't lonely; I just didn't know how to approach people and felt different from the other kids.

How I behave is different from how I want to behave — and the test only captures one of those things.

I wasn't completely different from everyone, but when I took the test I was somewhat influenced by my desires. My confirmation bias led me to pick the responses I wanted rather than the ones that reflected reality. So I took the test and came out as introverted and ethical — the exact opposite of how I am now.

I'm not a cold-hearted person, though. These things work like this: you naturally relate to the world in your own way and unconsciously assume everyone else thinks just like you — but that's not true, because people fall into distinct categories that can be clearly separated.

Two Fundamental Axes

Two major categories we can divide people into are Intuitive and Sensing, and two more are Ethical and Rational. Every person combines one from each pair — the interplay is what makes typology genuinely interesting.

Perception axis

Intuitive

Sees things not as they are but as what they could or couldn't be. Thinks in potential, abstract patterns, and future possibility.

Perception axis

Sensing

Sees things as they are. Focuses on the tangible, physical world — what is present and verifiable right now.

Judgement axis

Ethical

Categorises people through interpersonal dynamics. Senses whether someone likes you or not; prioritises human relationships over abstraction.

Judgement axis

Rational

Organises things into logical systems. Asks whether statement X follows from statement Y — coherence and structure over sentiment.

The judging axis always pairs with the perception axis in one way or another, but it concerns how we process information and interpret it from a subjective point of view. Together, these four poles form the skeleton of socionics — and, arguably, of how we understand one another at all.
